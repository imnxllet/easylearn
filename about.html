What We Do

We provide summer camp for students that wish to explore other option other their country. The goal is to allow students to gain industry insights that fit their interests as well as understanding the value of Canada. We do so by allowing these students to meet professionals in the industry, explore company building and create workshop that benefit their personal growth. By the end of the camp, students should have great knowledge towards the subject taught in the camp (depends on the type of camp the students choose).

Our Team


Hugo Chang

The founder of University of Toronto International Cantonese Union(UTICU). In the past 3 years, he actively participated in the event of growing UTICU. The club has had exponential growth and it is now serving more than 600 students at UofT. He created a enthusiastic environment in UofT for which VISA students from China feel belonged and welcomed. His understandings for the needs of chinese students are developed through his past experience dealing with chinese VISA students. In the past, he participated in planning an orientation event which allow students to explore the university before class starts. As a result, students gained more understanding towards their interests and lifestyle of a UofT student. His background also allowed him to develop sharp insight in the needs of VISA students. He grew up in Canada with a chinese background that helps him in acknowledging the value of chinese people and the social norm of canadian.



Natalie Deng

Natalie is an international student at University of Toronto Mississauga. She is working towards a specialist degree in digital enterprise management. She is also the founder and current president of UTM CHANNEL6ix film club. Founded in 2016, CHNNEL6ix has grown to 800 members and holds a variety of events monthly. Starting and growing an organization give her experience in communicating with administration and accommodating the needs of visa students. She hopes to bridge Chinese and western cultures while providing attentive service to a Chinese group.
